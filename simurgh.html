The simurgh is a helpful and beneficent giant bird from Persian mythology.

The simurgh appears in Iranian myths of all periods, as well as in the folklore of Armenia, Georgia in Eastern Europe, and the Byzantine Empire. All of these areas were under Persian influence at one time.

The simurgh is a giant bird with the head of a dog and lions' claws. Sometimes it has a human face. It's wings are the color of copper.

The simurgh is usually depicted as female.

It's big enough to carry off an elephant or a whale. Like the Jewish Ziz, its wingspan is supposed to be large enough to blot out the sun.

The simurgh is reputed to have an extremely long lifespan. Some legends say it lives 1,700 years.
