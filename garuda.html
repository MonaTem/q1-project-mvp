The Garuda is a large legendary bird, half god and half beast. It appears in Hindu myths in many of the countries of Southeast Asia and its environs.

The Garuda figures prominently as a cultural symbol in the following countries: Thailand, India, Indonesia, Japan, Suriname, Myanmar, and Mongolia.

The Garuda has a humanoid upper body, face, and arms and the lower body of an eagle. However, it also has the talons, wings, and beak of an eagle affixed to its humanoid face and arms.

The Garuda was favored mount of Lord Vishnu, one of the main Hindu gods.

The Garuda had great power. It could create storms with its wings and fly above the clouds for long distances.

The Garuda is also depicted as wise and gentle.

British fantasy writer China Mieville includes a Garuda as an important character in his classic "Perdido Street Station". The Garuda, Yagharek, has been exiled from his people and had his wings removed as a punishment. He is a poignant character.
