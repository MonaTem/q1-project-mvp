The Leviathan is a gigantic sea creature referred to in the Hebrew Old Testament's books of Job, Psalms, Amos, and Isaiah.

It's also indirectly referenced in Revelations in the New Testament.

Leviathan resembles a huge ocean dwelling dragon.

Leviathan appears to be derived from the older Canaanite myth of Lotan, a similar monster.

An early Rabbi, Rabbi Johanan, alive during the Roman empire described Leviathan eating a fish that was three hundred miles long.
