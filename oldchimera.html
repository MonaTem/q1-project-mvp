The Chimera is a monster from Greek mythology. It's usually shown as a lion with a goat's head arising from it's back and one or many tails terminating in snakes heads.

The word "chimera" means "she-goat " in Greek

Various parentages are suggested for the Chimera. In one, its mother is Echidna (half snake and half woman) and it's father is Typhon, a monstrous serpentine giant. There are various descriptions of Chimera's dad, but many show him with a human head and wings.

Chimera can also be a catchall term for any strange monster composed of parts of different animals or for anything fabulous, imaginary or unlikely.
