The Loch Ness Monster (or Nessie) is a Scottish legend and possibly the world's most famous contemporary monster.

The Loch Ness Monster is a large serpentine creature that is reputed to live in Loch Ness, a "loch" (lake) in the Scottish Highlands in the north of Scotland.

The first reported sighting of the Loch Ness Monster was in the sixth century by Irish monk Saint Columba and a group of people with him. The saint supposedly spoke to the monster and asked it to go away and leave them alone. Nessie is supposed to have obeyed him.

In the twentieth and twenty-first centuries there have been many sightings of the monster, some in the Loch and at least one on land.
