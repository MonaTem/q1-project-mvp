Behemoth and Leviathan, William Blake

The Behemoth is a beast mentioned in the "Book of Job" in the Old Testament.

Behemoth is a bit mysterious. Descriptions of it vary. It could be a mythical creature, a rhino, a hippo, a buffalo, an elephant, or a dinosaur.

The "beast from the earth" mentioned in "Revelations" in the New Testament isn't called Behemoth, but it sounds very similar. It is called a "false prophet" and its purpose is to promote worship of the Beast from the Sea.

Here is a description of Behemoth from Job 40:15-24 King James Version:

15 Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox.

16 Lo now, his strength is in his loins, and his force is in the navel of his belly.

17 He moveth his tail like a cedar: the sinews of his stones are wrapped together.

18 His bones are as strong pieces of brass; his bones are like bars of iron.

23 Behold, he drinketh up a river, and hasteth not: he trusteth that he can draw up Jordan into his mouth.

24 He taketh it with his eyes: his nose pierceth through snares.
